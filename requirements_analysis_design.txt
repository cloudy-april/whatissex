0) ASSIGNMENT CONTEXT (CS 3215 — HUMAN COMPUTER INTERACTION)
- Institution: CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES, College of Computer Studies (Republic of the Philippines, Nabua, Camarines Sur).
- Course/Task: Learning Task on Interaction Design Basics for CS 3215 (Human Computer Interaction).
- Team: BSCS-3A (Group #6) — Jave A. Bacsain, John Louie B. Bagaporo, Frederick D. Ortinero, Marc Justin N. Prestado.
- Baseline assumption: The original Arngren site (cluttered collage) is the starting point; Arngren Plus is the improved experience we are documenting and implementing.

1) PROBLEM STATEMENT AND GOALS
- The legacy Arngren site functions like a single cluttered bulletin board: hundreds of overlapping product images, inconsistent colors, no grid, and unclear link targets. Users must scroll and hope to stumble onto the right item; even then link targets are unpredictable.
- Primary audiences: hobbyists, gadget enthusiasts, ATV/scooter/RC shoppers, and curious international visitors (primarily Norway-based, but globally curious) using desktop, mobile, and assistive tech.
- Design pillars from the brief: Organization, Clarity, Functionality, Responsiveness.
- Additional design goals drawn from the Interaction Design task:
  - Organization: predictable categories, navigation, and layout.
  - Clarity: readable hierarchy, consistent text/image treatment, and trustworthy product details.
  - Functionality: search/filter, cart, shipping estimation, and secure checkout.
  - Responsiveness: layouts that hold up on phones and tablets.

2) CURRENT EXPERIENCE (PAIN POINTS)
- Manual scavenging: Users scroll a collage without a homepage, hero, or categories.
- Uncertain actions: Links and buttons are unpredictable; shipping and payment flows are opaque.
- Missing structure: No keyword search, filters, or sort; no cart or saved state for multi-item orders.
- Mobile breakage: Columns and tiny text fail on small screens.

3) REQUIREMENTS FOR ARNGREN PLUS
- Provide a welcoming homepage with featured/seasonal items and immediate entry points to categories.
- Add structured navigation: persistent top nav, category list, breadcrumbs, and predictable link targets.
- Deliver strong search/filter/sort for rapid item discovery across categories.
- Standardize product detail pages with gallery, specs, availability, pricing, reviews, and “what’s in the box.”
- Support a persistent cart (multi-item, quantity edits, save-for-later) that keeps state across pages.
- Show shipping estimation early (location + method) so totals are transparent before payment.
- Ensure secure checkout with OTP confirmation (6-digit code, 5-minute expiry, 5-attempt limit, 10-minute lockout on failure).
- Make the full flow responsive and accessible (touch targets, legible type, contrast, and focus states).

4) ANALYSIS — 8 ISSUE SCENARIOS IN THE LEGACY SITE
- 1. Visual overload: Dense collage causes choice paralysis and missed products.
- 2. No structured navigation: Users cannot jump to RC, drones, tools, or vehicles and quickly bounce.
- 3. Weak search/filter: No keyword search, filters, or sort; users manually scan dozens of items.
- 4. Inconsistent product info: Specs, pricing, and availability are unclear, leading to wrong purchases or support tickets.
- 5. No cart or saved state: Buyers can only order one item at a time and re-enter shipping/payment data, inflating effort and shipping fees.
- 6. Hidden shipping costs: Costs are revealed late (or offline), causing abandonment when totals rise.
- 7. Uncertain payment security: No visible verification step; users worry about fraud or mistyped orders.
- 8. Poor mobile/responsive experience: Columns and tiny text break on phones, blocking on-the-go shopping.
- 9. Inconsistent catalog design: Categories, labels, and layouts change per page, mixing promos with products and duplicating groups, so users cannot predict where an item lives or what a card will do.

5) DESIGN RESPONSES IN THE ARNGREN PLUS MOCK-UPS
- Solution to 1 (Visual overload): A real homepage with hero, featured items, and grid spacing introduces the catalog gradually before deep browsing.
- Solution to 2 (No navigation): A persistent top nav and side category list let users jump directly to product groups; breadcrumbs keep location clear; links have predictable targets. The nav also answers the brief’s “Poor Navigation” fix with categories (Electronics, Vehicles, Gadgets) plus a search bar.
- Solution to 3 (Weak search/filter): Global search with autocomplete plus filters (price, rating, stock, category) and sort controls surface the right items quickly.
- Solution to 4 (Inconsistent info): Standardized product detail pages show gallery, specs table, compatibility, reviews, and “what’s in the box,” reducing bad orders.
- Solution to 5 (No cart): A persistent cart with quantity edits, save-for-later, and mini-cart preview lets users bundle items and keep state across pages.
- Solution to 6 (Hidden shipping): Cart and checkout include a shipping estimator (location + method) before payment, showing totals and fees upfront.
- Solution to 7 (Payment security): Checkout sends a 6-digit OTP to the registered email/number; the user must confirm before charging. OTPs expire after 5 minutes, attempts are rate-limited (5 tries) before a 10-minute lockout, and error states for expired, wrong, or missing OTP are shown with retry/resend.
- Solution to 8 (Mobile/responsive): Responsive grid, legible typography, and touch-friendly buttons ensure the flows (browse, cart, OTP) work on phones and tablets.
- Solution to 9 (Inconsistent catalog design): A consistent taxonomy with well-defined categories, standardized card and detail templates, and clear breadcrumb/nav patterns keep categories, promos, and external links separate. Users can predict where items belong and what a click will do.

6) FLOW MAP (SEE MOCK-UPS)
- Direct search → product detail → add to cart → shipping estimation → OTP-confirmed checkout.
- Category browse → compare specs → cart → shipping estimator → secure payment.
- Cart management → shipping calculation → OTP confirmation screens with explicit success/error handling.
