ARNGREN REQUIREMENTS, ANALYSIS, AND DESIGN (ARNGREN PLUS)
Reference mock-up: https://mprestado.github.io/arngren-plus/

1) REQUIREMENTS
- Current situation: The legacy Arngren site is cluttered, has no real homepage or menu, and forces buyers to scroll a wall of products with little structure. Shipping and payment steps are unclear.
- What is needed: A structured e-commerce flow with a welcoming homepage, clear categories, strong search/filter, consistent product details, a persistent cart, and a secure, transparent checkout (shipping estimation + OTP confirmation).
- Who are the users: directed buyers who know the exact item, hobbyists/explorers comparing gadgets, bargain hunters checking deals, and first-time visitors using desktop or mobile (including accessibility users).
- How they currently shop: Mostly desktop browsing a collage of items, copying details into emails or single-item order forms. There is no multi-item cart, no real-time shipping calculator, and no guided payment confirmation.
- How they should be able to shop: Enter via a homepage with featured/seasonal items, browse categories or search with filters, open product pages with specs/reviews, add multiple items to a cart, estimate shipping early, and complete checkout with an OTP to verify payment.

2) ANALYSIS — 8 ISSUE SCENARIOS IN THE CURRENT PROCESS
1. Visual overload: Dense collage causes choice paralysis and missed products.
2. No structured navigation: Users cannot jump to a category (RC, drones, tools); they bounce after hunting.
3. Weak search/filter: No keyword search, filters, or sort; users manually scan dozens of items.
4. Inconsistent product info: Specs, pricing, and availability are unclear, leading to wrong purchases or support tickets.
5. No cart or saved state: Buyers can only order one item at a time and re-enter shipping/payment data, inflating effort and shipping fees.
6. Hidden shipping costs: Costs are revealed late (or offline), causing abandonment when totals rise.
7. Uncertain payment security: No visible verification step; users worry about fraud or mistyped orders.
8. Poor mobile/responsive experience: Columns and tiny text break on phones, blocking on-the-go shopping.

3) DESIGN — HOW THE ARNGREN PLUS MOCK-UPS SOLVE EACH ISSUE
- Solution to 1 (Visual overload): A real homepage with hero, featured items, and grid spacing introduces the catalog gradually before deep browsing.
- Solution to 2 (No navigation): A persistent top nav and side category list let users jump directly to product groups; breadcrumbs keep location clear.
- Solution to 3 (Weak search/filter): Global search with autocomplete plus filters (price, rating, stock, category) and sort controls surface the right items quickly.
- Solution to 4 (Inconsistent info): Standardized product detail pages show gallery, specs table, compatibility, reviews, and “what’s in the box,” reducing bad orders.
- Solution to 5 (No cart): A persistent cart with quantity edits, save-for-later, and mini-cart preview lets users bundle items and keep state across pages.
- Solution to 6 (Hidden shipping): Cart and checkout include a shipping estimator (location + method) before payment, showing totals and fees upfront.
- Solution to 7 (Payment security): Checkout sends a 4-digit OTP to the registered email/number; the user must confirm before charging. Error states for expired, wrong, or missing OTP are shown with retry/resend.
- Solution to 8 (Mobile/responsive): Responsive grid, legible typography, and touch-friendly buttons ensure the flows (browse, cart, OTP) work on phones and tablets.

How flows map to mock-ups (see link above):
- Direct search → product detail → add to cart → OTP-confirmed checkout.
- Category browse → compare specs → cart → shipping estimator → secure payment.
- Cart management → shipping calculation → OTP confirmation screens with explicit success/error handling.
